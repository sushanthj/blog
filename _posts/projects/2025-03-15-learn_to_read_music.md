A browser-based app for learning to sight-read music notation across treble, bass, and grand staff. It adapts to focus on the notes you struggle with most.

[Try it live](https://sushanthj.github.io/learn_to_read_music/) | [GitHub](https://github.com/sushanthj/learn_to_read_music)

![](/images/projects/learn_to_read_music/cover.gif)

Notes appear on the staff and you identify them. The app tracks your accuracy per note — ones you miss appear 2-4x more often. Score 85%+ on your last 10 attempts to unlock the next level.

You can plug in a MIDI keyboard for exact pitch matching, or use your microphone — the app does real-time pitch detection so you can practice with your actual guitar or piano instead of clicking buttons. In guitar mode, tab hints show you exactly where to fret each note.

![](/images/projects/learn_to_read_music/guitar_mic.gif)


Built with vanilla JS + VexFlow 5. No frameworks, no build step, zero dependencies.
